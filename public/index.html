  • Making Changes

  • Let's say you're driving in the car, and you just caught the end of a fantastic song on the radio. How do you find out what it was?

    You can wait half an hour for the DJ to come back on and announce the title. Or a passenger (eyes on the road, you!) can mark what station you're on by tapping its name in Just Played.

    Later, when you're back home, you can look up all the songs with the touch of a button, and tap a song title to buy it from the iTunes Store.

    1. The first time you launch Just Played, tap the Reload button in the corner. The app will connect to the network and fill in the list of supported radio stations.
    2. Any time you hear a song you like, tap the name of the radio station to make a "snap" of that moment in time. If you're driving, have someone else do this for you!
    3. When you're back on your home WiFi connection, click the Reload button. The app will look up the title and artist for each snap, and show them in place of the radio station call letters. If a song title fails to appear, it means that the information was unavailable from the network.
    4. Tap a song title to search for it in the iTunes Store.
    5. Press the Delete button to clear out the list of snaps and make room for more.
    6. If the radio station you like isn't on the list, see the "Making Changes" page of this guide.
  • Radio Stations

  • In the somewhat likely case that the radio station you want isn't on the list, you can tell Just Played to look up stations and songs from a different network server. Press your iPhone's Home button, tap Settings, and go to the Just Played section to change servers.

    Where do you find one that has your favorite radio station? See below.

  • Servers

  • Either a friend has set up a server somewhere and given you the address, or... you're going to have to be that friend. The good news is that this task is well within your grasp. All the source code to the demonstration server is available in the official repository, along with instructions on how to add new stations.
